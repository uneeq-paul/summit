# Summit — Design Notes

*Working notes for the designer — not part of the player rulebook. This is where
ideas that aren't in the game yet, and questions to settle through playtesting,
are kept.*

## Ideas Parking Lot

Things that could be added later, once the core game has been played a few times.

- **New biomes.** Reaching the summit could open a new biome with fresh positive
  and negative effects. Start with a single biome (start to summit) and add more
  once the base game feels right.
- **Weather / turn events.** A shared event at the start of each round that
  affects everyone — e.g. a storm turn where all climbs cost +2, or a clear turn
  with no hunger. Gives the mountain its own "voice" and a home for the cold
  theme beyond hard mode.
- **Rest action.** Spend a whole turn stationary to recover. May be redundant
  with the Healer and food — test whether the cube economy needs another recovery
  lever.
- **Campfire item.** A reusable item that warms everyone on its hex each turn.
  Worth adding if hard-mode cold turns out to force the team together too rigidly
  — it lets a scout buy some time alone.

## Questions to Settle in Playtesting

- **Hex numbers.** The single most important thing to lock down. The whole stamina
  spread (12–20) assumes a typical climb requirement of about 10–14. Decide what
  numbers go on the hex edges first — everything else calibrates off that.
- **Class stamina balance.** Current spread: Agile 20, Climber 18,
  Healer/Cook/Strong 16, Gambler/Android 14. Watch the low-pool classes — lift
  them by 2 if they pass out constantly. If the Agile feels strictly best, trim it
  toward 18–19 (doubled item weight may be a softer cost than lost stamina).
- **Agile movement.** The Agile moves two hexes per turn, everyone else one. If
  two feels too limited in play, try allowing three — but watch that the Agile
  doesn't range so far ahead that it breaks up the group and erodes the buddy
  bonuses.
- **Healer strength.** Full-roll healing may be too strong. If so, cap it (e.g.
  heal no more than 4) or add a cost (can't climb the turn it heals) before
  reducing the roll.
- **Cold and togetherness.** Watch whether the team ever meaningfully separates in
  hard mode. If cold makes staying together the only correct choice, add the
  campfire so solitude becomes a buyable resource.
- **Summit distance.** Set to 20 hexes. Adjust for game length once you know how
  long an average turn takes.
- **Card counts and weights.** Deck is currently 73 cards. Rebalance counts once
  you see which items are drawn too often or too rarely.
- **Lose-by-attrition.** Currently you only lose if all players pass out at once.
  Decide whether a stalled team (unable to progress) should also lose, or whether
  passing out is enough.
